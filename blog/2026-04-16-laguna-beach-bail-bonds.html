Where Laguna Beach Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Laguna Beach is transported to the Laguna Beach Police Department Jail at 505 Forest Avenue, Laguna Beach, CA 92651 (phone: 949-497-0701). The LBPD Jail is a small city-PD facility in downtown Laguna Beach, co-located with police headquarters and just up the hill from Main Beach and PCH.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Laguna Beach in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Laguna Beach has its own small police department and a city jail at 505 Forest Avenue, in downtown Laguna Beach just up the hill from Main Beach and Pacific Coast Highway. LBPD handles local bookings; the jail is sized for a 23,000-resident city but processes outsized arrest volume because of the six million annual visitors who pass through downtown and along PCH.

The geography is the story. Laguna Beach stretches along seven miles of coastline, connected by Pacific Coast Highway as the main north-south artery. Canyon Road, Laguna Canyon Road (the 133), and PCH are the only real routes in and out. This means LBPD's DUI enforcement is highly concentrated on PCH, with stops regularly happening near Main Beach, the intersection with Broadway, and the Crystal Cove entrance north of town. Summer arts-festival season — Sawdust Festival, Pageant of the Masters, Art-A-Fair — drives a predictable weekend surge in bar-district and PCH arrests from June through August.

The LBPD jail runs small, which cuts both ways. Misdemeanor bonds often post in 1–3 hours with fast release. But when the jail is at capacity during festival season or major events, overflow transfers to the Orange County IRC in Santa Ana happen faster than usual — sometimes within 2–3 hours of initial booking. The window to post locally is tight on busy summer weekends. Angels Bail Bonds monitors the jail population directly.

How a Laguna Beach Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — laguna beach bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at LBPD Jail and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Laguna Beach arrest actually get processed?

Laguna Beach arrests are processed at the LBPD Jail at 505 Forest Avenue in downtown Laguna Beach. Misdemeanor bonds post here with 1–3 hour release typical. Summer festival season increases transfer volume to the Orange County IRC in Santa Ana.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Laguna Beach?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Laguna Beach bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Laguna Beach DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
