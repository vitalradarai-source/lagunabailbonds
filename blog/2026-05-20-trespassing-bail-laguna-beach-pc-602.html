Laguna Beach-Specific Facts

PC §602 has over 30 sub-sections covering different trespass scenarios. The most-used ones in Laguna Beach: §602(k) entering land for harassment or annoyance; §602(l) entering for the purpose of injuring property; §602(m) entering and refusing to leave; §602(o) entering posted private property; §602(s) entering property of a public school. Each sub-section has different elements and different probable-cause requirements.

Laguna Beach's coastal-access controversy creates a §602 enforcement pattern unique to the city. California's Coastal Act guarantees public beach access, but private beachfront homeowners often post "no trespassing" signs above the mean high tide line. Tourists/locals walking on the wet sand below the line are legally protected; those who cross above the line onto dry sand are technically trespassing under §602(o). LBPD enforces inconsistently — usually warning first, citing repeat offenders.

The OC bail schedule sets PC §602 misdemeanor at $500. Bond cost at 10% statutory premium = $50. Most §602 arrests in Laguna Beach resolve at the PD via PC §853.6 cite-and-release without bail being posted. When bail IS posted, it's usually because of stacked charges — §602 + PC §148(a) resisting + PC §415 disturbing the peace, which together push bail to $2,500-$5,000.

Defense strategy in Laguna §602 cases often focuses on the property-owner consent question. Many alleged trespass cases involve historic public access (paths used for decades) or coastal-access easements that were never properly recorded. A property-record search before arraignment can sometimes establish that the location wasn't legally "private" in the first place, defeating the trespass element entirely.

What PC §602 Actually Says

Penal Code §602: Trespassing — misdemeanor with multiple sub-elements including beach access disputes. Understanding the statute matters because bail, no-bail rules, and protective orders all flow from charging decisions.

Bail Cost and the 10% Premium

The 10% statutory premium under California Insurance Code §1800.4 is a fee for the surety service, not money lent. Non-refundable regardless of case outcome. No licensed bondsman can lawfully charge a different rate.

Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Don't wait for arraignment — bail can post once booking is complete.
  2. Don't pay cash bail without checking with a bondsman — ties up full amount for 6-18 months.
  3. Don't sign indemnitor paperwork unread — it's a binding contract.
  4. Don't coach the defendant on jail calls — they're recorded.
  5. Don't violate any protective order, even verbally — PC §166 stacks fast.
