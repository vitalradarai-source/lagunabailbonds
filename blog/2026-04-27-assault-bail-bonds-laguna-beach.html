Understanding California Assault Charges

California separates assault from battery — a distinction that surprises most people:

"Wobbler" means the prosecutor can file the charge as either a misdemeanor or a felony depending on circumstances — prior record, severity of injury, weapon involved, victim vulnerability. The charge filing decision affects bail amount significantly.

Typical Bail Amounts in Orange County

Orange County publishes a bail schedule that lists default amounts for each charge. A judge can adjust higher or lower based on circumstances. Approximate ranges as of 2026:

  • PC 240 simple assault — $500 to $5,000
  • PC 242 battery — $5,000 to $10,000
  • PC 243(d) battery with serious injury — $25,000 to $50,000
  • PC 243(e)(1) domestic battery — $10,000 to $50,000
  • PC 245(a)(1) ADW — $50,000 to $100,000
  • PC 245(a)(2) ADW with firearm — $100,000 to $250,000+
  • PC 273.5 corporal injury — $50,000+

The 10% bond premium under California Insurance Code §1800.4 applies regardless of the bail amount — a $50,000 bail requires a $5,000 premium for the bondsman's surety bond. Payment plans are available for higher amounts.

What Happens After a Laguna Beach Assault Arrest

  1. Arrest by Laguna Beach PD. Officers transport the suspect to Laguna Beach Police Department, 505 Forest Ave, Laguna Beach, CA 92651, (949) 497-0701, for initial processing.
  2. Booking at LBPD. Fingerprints, photos, identity verification, charge entry. Typically 2 to 8 hours.
  3. Transfer to OC Central Jail. Most defendants are transferred within 24 to 48 hours to the OC Intake/Release Center at 550 N The City Dr, Orange, CA 92868.
  4. Bail set. For misdemeanors, bail follows the OC schedule automatically. For felonies (PC 245, PC 273.5), bail typically requires a judge's hearing — usually within 48 hours of arrest.
  5. Bond posted. Once bail is set, Angels Bail Bonds posts the surety bond at the holding facility. Call (626) 478-1062 — we can pre-qualify the cosigner before booking is complete to minimize total custody time.
  6. Release. Jail processing takes 2-6 additional hours after the bond is posted. Total custody time for a misdemeanor with prepared bond paperwork: typically 6-12 hours from arrest.
  7. Arraignment. The defendant's first court appearance is at the Harbor Justice Center (4601 Jamboree Rd, Newport Beach) for misdemeanors, or Central Justice Center (700 Civic Center Dr W, Santa Ana) for felonies. Within 48 hours of arrest excluding weekends.

Criminal Protective Orders and Bail

California courts often issue a Criminal Protective Order (CPO) under Penal Code §136.2 alongside assault charges, especially when victims and defendants know each other. The CPO typically restricts:

  • Contact with the alleged victim (in person, phone, text, social media, third-party messages)
  • Return to a shared residence
  • Possession of firearms during pendency

Critical clarification: PC 136.2 is a Criminal Protective Order, not a bail hold. Bail can be posted immediately after booking — the CPO restricts contact and behavior, not release from custody. Violating a CPO is a separate misdemeanor or felony under PC 273.6 carrying additional jail time and fines.

Self-Defense and What to Tell Investigators

This is critical: do not discuss the incident with police investigators without an attorney present. California recognizes self-defense (CALCRIM 3470 jury instruction), defense of others, and defense of property — but those are legal defenses presented in court, not statements that help during booking. Anything said to officers can be used against the defendant. The right to remain silent under the Fifth Amendment applies fully — exercise it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between assault and battery in California?

Assault (PC 240) is the unlawful attempt to use force — no contact required. Battery (PC 242) is the actual use of force. A swung fist that misses is assault; one that connects is battery.

How much is bail for assault in Laguna Beach?

Per OC bail schedule: PC 240 ~$500-$5,000; PC 242 ~$5,000-$10,000; PC 245(a)(1) ADW ~$50,000-$100,000; PC 245(a)(2) firearm ~$100,000+. Confirm actual amount with the booking facility.

What happens after the arrest?

LBPD books at 505 Forest Ave, transfers to OC Central Jail (550 N The City Dr, Orange) within 24-48hr. Misdemeanor bail set per OC schedule; felony bail at judge's hearing within 48hr.

Can bail be denied for assault?

Bail is available as a matter of right under CA Constitution Art I §12 for most charges. Exceptions include PC 245 felonies with strikes and cases where the prosecution proves substantial threat to public safety.

Does a Criminal Protective Order block bail?

No. PC 136.2 is a Criminal Protective Order — it restricts contact, not release. Bail can be posted immediately after booking. Violating the CPO is a separate offense under PC 273.6.

Should I hire a criminal defense attorney?

Yes — strongly recommended for any charge above PC 240. Felony assault can carry state prison time, strike convictions, and lifetime firearm prohibitions.
